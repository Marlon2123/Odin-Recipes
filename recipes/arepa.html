Venezuelan Arepas

This recipe details how to make delicious Arepas, a traditional Venezuelan dish. It starts by combining cornmeal, water, and salt to form a dough. After resting, the dough is divided and shaped into flat discs. These are then cooked in a skillet with butter until golden and crispy. The recipe suggests various fillings like cheese, shredded meat, or beans, and offers tips for customization and achieving a more authentic flavor.

step-by-step

Ingridients
  • Cornmeal
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter

Instructions

Step 1
  1. In a large bowl, combine the cornmeal, water, and salt.
  2. Mix thoroughly until a smooth, lump-free dough is formed.
  3. Let the dough rest at room temperature for about 5 minutes.
Step 2
  1. Divide the dough into 18 equal portions.
  2. Shape each portion into a disc about 3 inches in diameter and 1/4 inch thick.
Step 3
  1. Heat a skillet over medium-low heat and grease lightly with butter.
  2. Fry the arepas in batches until golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  3. Serve hot with your favorite filling.
Tips
  • You can add a little olive oil to the dough for a richer flavor.
  • If the dough is too dry, add a little more water. If it is too wet, add a little more cornmela
  • You can use a griddle to cook the arepas instead of a skillet.
  • For a more authentic flavor, cook the arepas on a budare (a cast iron griddle).
Fillings
