Peruvian Ceviche: A Feast of Fresh Flavors

Peruvian ceviche is an explosion of freshness and flavor that conquers palates. This traditional recipe combines the softness of white fish with the acidity of lime juice, thinly sliced red onion, chopped cilantro, and the spicy touch of aji.

step-by-step

Ingridients
  • 1 kg of white fish (sea bass, sole, grouper)
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Â¼ cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 yellow aji pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 1 aji limo pepper, chopped (for garnish)
  • 12 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1
  1. Wash the diced fish and onion in cold water. Dry them well with paper towels.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the fish, onion, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. If you want a spicier touch, add the chopped yellow aji pepper.
Step 2
  1. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for 20 minutes.
  2. Garnish with chopped cilantro and aji limo pepper to taste.
Tips
  • You can use different types of white fish, such as sea bass, sole, or grouper.
  • For a milder ceviche, you can use less lemon juice.
  • To minimize the acidity of the lemon, add a few ice cubes to the mixture.
  • Ceviche can be accompanied with cancha (fried corn), choclo (boiled), or sweet potato (boiled or fried)
Enjoy this delicious Peruvian dish!
